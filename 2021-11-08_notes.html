Group Exercises


Week 3 Day 1


  1. Discuss the risks and benefits of purchasing Office 365 vs LibreOffice. List 3-5 factors that influence your decision.


  1. Many “authorities” define cyber (or information) security objective by the CIA (confidentiality, integrity, availability)  triad. Yet others refer to the AAA (authentication, authorization, accounting) objectives. Compare and contrast these two approaches. Are they fundamentally different?


  1. List the major elements of ecommerce and compare and contrast them with their physical counterparts. Identify 3-5 areas where the risks differ significantly in one paradigm versus the other.


  1. Identify 3-5 financial services that would not be practical or even possible in the pre technological world.


  1. Using the services above, determine which have greater risk--the electronic or physical--and why.



Week 1 Exercises


  1. Internet email protocol, smtp, celebrates its 40th anniversary. Discuss how you might design email today so that security is “baked in” rather than added on like frosting and sprinkles.


  1. Speaking of email, discuss why it is so often used for various attacks: phishing, trojans, viruses, and even worms. Are the solutions user education and/or technology. This is a related but distinct question from the previous.


  1. In the email signature protocol description Alice and Bob send their own certificates. Are there issues with this? What stops Eve from creating a certificate stating that she is Alice?


  1. Compare and contrast command line, scripting, and programming.


  1. The original (IBM) PC operating system ws command line execution of programs. Microsoft after a battle Bill Gates had with IBM created their own version, MS DOS. It was strictly a command line. Microsoft then introduced Windows. How does Windows differ from the command line and how do its cybersecurity risks differ? Are they greater or reduced?


Week 2 Exercises


  1. The sea of tools and terminology regarding threat and intrusion analysis is wide and deep. Discuss how you might help yourself and others learn and retain the information. How does this learning help a cybersecurity professional manage risk?


  1. The traditional cliche is that we, the good guys, need to be right all the time and the bad guys only have to be right once. Discuss if this is true. What would be a more accurate and manageable characterization of risk (the last word is a clue).


  1. Anthropologists and evolutionary biologists maintain that the human hand, which requires an enormous portion of the brain to operate, ultimately led to our dominance over all fauna. It was of course the use of tools, and in particular, the ability to apply leverage that led to our success over animals. Discuss the analogous leverage that cybersecurity tools provide that helps us stay ahead of the threat actors.


  1. Discuss ways of securing endpoints that require no additional security from any other structural or OSI layer. What are the advantages and disadvantages of this approach?


  1. Reverse engineering is a tool for us to understand and defend against malware. It is also a tool for economic adversaries to steal intellectual property. Discuss the significance of that risk and how it might be mitigated.


  1. Tim Burnes-Lee developed the world wide web and HTTP in 1989 as a universal sharing protocol extending the reach of hypertext to the internet. Other protocols at the time were clumsy and limited. Discuss how HTTP introduced both great functionality and great risk to networked computers. Speculate whether increased functionality necessarily leads to increased risk and whether careful development might reduce that correlation.


  1. Percent encoding, SQL injections, cross site scripting, and their cousins, while exploiting structural computing weaknesses stemming from the Von Neumann architecture, they are not necessarily inevitable. Discuss controls that would reduce or eliminate attacks that poison tables and/or change instructions. How does the cliche An ounce of protection is worth a pound of cure apply?


  1. Discuss the trade offs in tuning detection and protection systems to recognize, warn, and possibly deter attacks.


  1. Firewalls, proxy servers, and IDS/IPS have much in common and significant differences. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of combining their functionality in a common device, or fewer devices.


  1. NAC is an approach, a generic product, and a specific commercial product. Discuss the similarities and differences between NAC and access control applied to computer systems. What makes NAC different and more challenging?


Week 3 Exercises


  1. Data exfiltration presents a significant challenge for DLP programs. Discuss why it is so hard and various means to manage its risk.


  1. Compare and contrast the “kill chain” vs the ATT&CK framework. Which do you prefer and why?


  1. As US Secretary of Defense, Donald Rumsfeld achieved significant notice and some notoriety for espousing the “unknown unknown” concept. While he applied it in geopolitics, cybersecurity professionals respect it as well. Discuss strategies for managing the risk of “unknown unknowns” and provide a recent example of one if you can.


  1. Threat hunting has become trendy in cybersecurity circles. Discuss how it differs from the traditional assessments like vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, reviews against frameworks, etc.


  1. What advantage does an adversary obtain by email harvesting and DNS harvesting? Which is more likely to succeed in compromise?


Week 4 Exercises


  1. What controls would you need to prevent a successful ransomware attack? Is it possible to prevent with near certainty a ransomware attack? Why or why not?


  1. When you walk by a wiring closet you notice that the door is ajar being propped open by an empty paint can. You can smell wet paint from inside the closet. What actions should you take as the cybersecurity officer? Who is ultimately responsible for the situation and what decision would you recommend that they make?


  1. Your organization requires two factor authentication. After internal users enter their username and password they are required to enter an 8 digit continually changing security code displayed on their smart card. A senior manager wants to allow the help desk to bypass the second factor if the employee calls up, states that they do not have the smart card currently, and “satisfies” the agent through personal questions that they are who they say they are? Make both cases for and against this protocol.



  1. Why are zero-day attacks so problematic? How do you defend against them?


  1. The global positioning system was developed by the US military towards the end of the 20th century and for a while gave them a significant tactical advantage. Nevertheless the US government opened up the GPS system to the entire world. Provide the pros and cons of this from the perspective of a cyber security professional.


Week 5 Exercises


  1. A SOC receives a call early Wednesday afternoon from the company’s physical security department that an executive’s laptop was stolen from their car Tuesday evening while they were dining in a restaurant. The department reports that the situation is low priority because the cost of the laptop was less than $1000 and it was insured. What are the risks? What process improvements do you recommend?


  1. You are tasked to review your company’s trouble ticket process. What tickets should be marked as urgent and reviewed by SOC or CSIRT personnel?


  1. The differences between the Core and Intermediate Stack are quantitative and qualitative. The quantitative drills deeper. How would you characterize the qualitative difference given today’s presentation?


  1. Describe some “moving parts” in a SOC and how and why they would need to mesh together.


  1. Control type characterizations are straightforward and almost everyone agrees on the three types. Why do you think that there are so many different mechanistic descriptions?


Week 5 Exercises


  1. The terms “elastic” and “plastic” have special meanings in materials science. Elastic deformations typically return to their original shape when stress is abated; plastic deformations do not. Give examples of “plastic” computer events or incidents. 


  1. Automation has benefits in many domains. Describe three IT automation scenarios and discuss the benefits and risks of each. (Example, automatic logout after n minutes of inactivity.)


  1. “Extensibility” is analogous to but different from scalability. The former means (in our context) the ability to add additional functionality. Compare and contrast this with scalability and with elasticity. Why is this understanding important in the cybersecurity domain?


  1. Parity is frequently used for error detection. Discuss its benefits and limitations. I asserted that its “cousins” can be used to correct errors. How would this be possible?


  1. Using high level descriptive terms (pseudo-code), describe an implementation that eliminates seek and latency time for all but the first RAID 0 file read for reading eight bytes off of . (Use terms like “position the read head at track n and hold” and “begin track access so that read head will be at byte x on track n when previous read is complete.” With what other RAID modes would this be effective.


  1. Propose a cluster failover process with the following. Several Windows Servers providing Google-like search capabilities on their web servers, accessed through a load balancer. Assume all components have mutual communication capabilities. The process must provide a seamless experience for the user. (Eg, the user would not be aware of any failover.)


Week 6 Exercises


  1. Backup systems are sometimes used as archives. Discuss whether this is a good idea and whether there needs to be further controls or actions to effectively implement backups as archives.


  1. Typical cloud backup services continually (or daily) backup files with the archive bit set. Oftentimes subscribers find that their backup storage is greater than their current storage. Why is this? What mechanisms do you think the cloud providers have to remedy this? Finally, what are the advantages of not using the remedy? disadvantages?


  1. The presentations have mentioned the Morris Worm at least four times in a few weeks. Discuss why this one event had such a significant impact on cybersecurity structure and practice. The firewall, for example, came into prominence and almost universal use because of the MW. Why?


  1. Beginning in early 1989 the DoD and DoE each provided 24x7x365 incident response for their constituents. If you were a member of the team and got a page at 3am Wednesday morning from a security officer at Hanford (a nuclear processing facility) who said that she believed that the computer that kept an inventory of low level nuclear waste had been compromised what action would you take?


  1. You’re the security officer of a mid-sized company that makes components for water treatment facilities. A system administrator contacts you and is “somewhat certain” that a computer containing confidential water quality status for a number of municipalities has been compromised. Create a list of stakeholders that should be notified and what action you would request those stakeholders take.


  1. You’re the security officer for a large corporation. The General Council (the top company attorney) informs you that the Federal District Court for Northern Virginia has subpoenaed “all computer records and logs related to any computer incidents regarding the accounts of the company’s 5 largest clients.” What procedures and processes would you have liked to have had in place to answer this subpoena?
