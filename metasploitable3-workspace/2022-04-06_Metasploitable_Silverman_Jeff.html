This report is divided into 2 parts: 1) an executive overview of the process I used to find the vulnerability and 2) details of what I did, what I found, and why I came to the conclusions I came to.

Windows Server/2008 (win2K8)

I used the nmap -sn command to find the win2k8 machine by running it once, then turning on the win2K8 machine, running nmap -sn again, and comparing the outputs.  The new entry was my victim.

I ran nmap with the -A option and I found that the Windows/2008 machine had the vulnerability CVE-2017-0143.  This vulnerability has a Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS) score of 8.1, high.
